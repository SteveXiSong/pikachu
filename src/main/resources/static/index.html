AI for everyone - How companies can benefit from the advance of machine learning

无处不在的人工智能 - 企业如何利用机器学习

By Werner Vogels on 04 September 2017 10:00 AM PDT

Translated By Steve Song on 04 September 2017 6:00 PM PDT

When a technology has its breakthrough, can often only be determined in hindsight. In the case of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML), this is different. ML is that part of AI that describes rules and recognizes patterns from large amounts of data in order to predict future data. Both concepts are virtually omnipresent and at the top of most buzzword rankings. Personally, I think – and this is clearly linked to the rise of AI and ML – that there has never been a better time than today to develop smart applications and use them. Why? Because three things are coming together. First: Users across the globe are capturing data digitally, whether this is in the physical world through sensors or GPS, or online through click stream data. As a result, there is a critical mass of data available. Secondly, there is enough affordable computing capacity in the cloud for companies and organizations, no matter what their size, to use intelligent applications. And thirdly, an "algorithmic revolution" has taken place, meaning it is now possible to train trillions of algorithms simultaneously, making the whole machine learning process much faster. This has allowed for more research, which has resulted in reaching the "critical mass" in knowledge that is needed to kick off an exponential growth in the development of new algorithms and architectures. We may have come a relatively long way with AI, but the progress came quietly. After all, during the last 50 years, AI and ML were fields that had only been accessible to an exclusive circle of researchers and scientists. That is now changing, as packages of AI and ML services, frameworks and tools are today available to all sorts of companies and organizations, including those that don't have dedicated research groups in this field. The management consultants at McKinsey expect that the global market for AI-based services, software and hardware will grow annually by 15-25% and reach a volume of around USD 130 billion in 2025. A number of start-ups are using AI algorithms for all things imaginable – searching for tumors in medical images, helping people learn foreign languages, or automating claims handling at insurance companies. At the same time, entirely new categories of applications are being created whereby a natural conversation between man and machine is taking center-stage.

人们通常事后才发现某项科技对于人类是多么地重要。然而对于人工智能和机器学习来说，这就有所不同了。作为人工智能的一个分支， 机器学习从大量的数据中描述一种规则和模式从而预测未来的数据。这两个名词基本上无处不在，而且占据了时髦词汇的头条。
我个人认为这明显的跟人工只能和机器学习的崛起有关。历史上从未有过比现在更好的时代人们开发智能应用并且使用它们。 为什么呢？有三个条件。第一，全球的用户都在产生数字化的数据。无论是显示世界中的传感器还是GPS，亦或是网络中的鼠标点击产生的数据流。 其结果就是我们有大量可获得的关键数据。第二，不管科技公司的规模大小，它们可以从云上获得足够的计算资源来让它们的应用运行。 第三，“算法革命”正在发生。这使得同时训练万亿级别的模型成为可能。这就让整个机器学习过程大大加快。同时这也可以激发更多的研究项目。 这些研究项目可以获得那些至关重要的大数据来应对指数级增长的新算法和架构。
我们在人工智能的道路上或许还有很长的路要走，但是过程在悄悄地发展。毕竟，在过去50年中，人工智能和机器学习领域仅仅存在于研究者和科学家的小圈子中。 然而现在变了。各种人工智能和机器学期的库、框架和工具对于各类公司和组织都是可以获得的，包括那些自己没有在专属领域有研究团队的公司。 麦肯锡的管理咨询团队预估全球市场上基于人工智能的服务、软件和硬件会每年以15-25%的速度增长，并且在2025年会达到1300亿的规模。 有相当数量的创业团队在使用人工智能算法在各种可以想象得到的事情上。从医疗图像的肿瘤诊断到帮助人们学习外语，再到保险公司自动化 处理申报事故。同时，各种全新的应用正在被发明。这也使得人与机器的对话成为了万众瞩目的焦点。

Progress through machine learning

Is the hype surrounding AI and ML even justified? Definitely, because they offer business and society fascinating possibilities. With the help of digitization and high-performance computers, we are able to replicate human intelligence in some areas, such as computer vision, and even surpass the intelligence of humans. We are creating very diverse algorithms for a wide range of application areas and turning these individual pieces into services so that ML is available for everyone. Packaged into applications and business models, ML can make our life more pleasant or safer. Take autonomous driving: 90% of car accidents in the US can be traced to "human failure". The assumption is that the number of accidents will decline over the long term if vehicles drive autonomously. In aviation, this has already been reality for a long time. MIT pioneers Erik Brynjolfsson and Andrew McAfee predict that the macroeconomic effect of the so-called "second machine age" will be comparable to what the steam engine once unleashed when it replaced humans' muscular strength ("the first machine age"). Many are uncomfortable with the idea that an artificial intelligence exists alongside human intelligence. That is understandable. We must therefore discuss – parallel to the technological developments – how humans and AI can co-exist in the future; the moral and ethical aspects that arise; how to ensure we have a good grip on AI; and which legal parameters we need in order to manage all this. Answering these questions will be just as important as the effort to solve the technological challenges, and neither dogmas nor ideologies will help. Instead, what's needed is an objective, broad-based debate that takes into account the wellbeing of society as a whole.

机器学习的发展

这些有关人工智能和机器学习的夸张宣传到底合理吗？答案是肯定的。因为它们给企业和社会提供了迷人的可能。 借助于数字化和高效能的计算机，人类已经可以在某些领域复制人类的智能，比如计算机视觉甚至超过了人类的能力。 我们正在广阔的范围内创造各种各样的应用，并且让这些个体集合成每个人都可以接触到的机器学习服务。 通过将它们打包成应用和商业模型，机器学习使我们的生活更美好更有保障。以自动驾驶举个例子：美国90%的交通事故最终原因 是“人为失误”。如果汽车是自动驾驶的，长远来看交通事故的数量是可以减少的。在航空领域，自动驾驶已经存在很长时间了。
MIT的前驱Erik Brynjolfsson和 Andrew McAfee预测这个被称为“第二个机器时代”的 宏观经济学效应将会与当初解放了人类肌肉力量的蒸汽机（“第一个机器时代”）相比拟。许多人还是对于人工智能与人类智能并存 的想法非常担心。这是可以理解的。因此，在科技发展的同时，我们必须讨论未来人类与人工智能的协作、道德与伦理的问题、 如果有效掌握人工智能以及我们需要哪些法律法规来管理这些问题。回答这些问题与有效解决技术难题一样重要。这并不是 单单学术专家与理想主义者就可以解决的。相反，我们需要的是考虑到全社会范围的客观的、广阔的讨论。

Machine Learning at Amazon

For the past 20 years, thousands of software engineers at Amazon have been working on ML. We dare to claim that we are the company that has been applying AI and ML as a business technology the longest. We know that innovative technologies always take off whenever barriers to entry fall for market participants. That is the case right now with AI and ML. In the past, anyone who wanted to use AI for himself had to start from scratch: develop algorithms and feed them with enormous amounts of data – even if he later needed an application for a strictly confined context. This is referred to as so-called "weak" AI. Many of the consumer interfaces that everyone is familiar with today, such as recommendations, similarities or autofill functions for search prediction – they are all ML driven. In the meantime, they can predict inventory levels or vendor lead times, detect customer problems and automatically deduct how to solve them; and discover counterfeit goods and sort out abusive reviews, thereby protecting our customers from fraud. But that is only the tip of the iceberg. At Amazon, we are sitting on billions of historical order information data, which allows us to create other AI/ML-based models based on AI for many different kinds of functionalities. For example programming interfaces that developers can use to analyze images, change text into true-to-life language or create chatbots. But ultimately, there is something to be found for everyone who wants to define models, train them, and then scale. Pre-configured, attuned libraries and deep learning frameworks are widely available, which allow anyone to get started very fast. Companies like Netflix, Nvidia, or Pinterest use our capabilities in ML and deep learning. More and more layers are being created in a kind of ecosystem on which companies and organizations can 'dock' their business – depending on how deep they want to, and are able to, immerse themselves in the subject matter. Decisive is the openness of the layers and the reliable availability of the infrastructure. In the past, AI technologies were so expensive that it was hardly worth it to use them. Today, AI and ML technologies are available off the shelf, and they can be called up according to one's individual requirements. They form the basis for new business models. Even users who are not AI specialists can very easily and affordably incorporate the building blocks into their own services. In particular small and medium-sized companies with innovative strength can benefit. They do not have to learn any complex ML algorithms and technologies, and they can experiment without incurring high costs.

亚马逊的机器学习

在过去20年内，成千的亚马逊工程师致力于机器学习。我们敢说我们是将人工智能和机器学习应用到商业上的历史最悠久的企业。 我们认为创新的科技总是可以解决市场玩家们的难题。
当今的人工智能和机器学习正是一个例子。过去，任何一个想要使用人工智能的人不得不从头开始：开发算法，然后导入海量的数据。 即使过后他不得不开发一个针对特定范围的应用。这被称作“弱人工智能”。现在很多客户知熟知的接口，如推荐系统、相似性或者搜索自动 补全功能，它们都是由机器学习驱动的。同时，它们也可以预测库存水平或供应商交货时间、感知客户问题以及自动推断怎样解决它们； 它们还可以发现假冒商品、分辨出恶评从而保护客户不受欺诈侵扰。然而，这些都只是冰山一角。在亚马逊，我们坐拥数十亿的 客户订单数据。这使得我们可以开发出各种各样基于人工智能的功能。比如，开发者可以分析图像、文本转换为真实语言 或者打造机器人。但是最终，每个需要建立模型、训练和规模化的用户都可以找到一些有用的东西。这些预先配置好的、调整过的 库或者机器学习框架让每个人都可以快速开发原形。
像Netflix、英伟达或者Pinterest这些公司，它们都在使用我们的机器学习和深度学习的功能。这个生态系统中越来越多的 层级被开发出来。根据有多深入的使用这些功能，这些企业可以将它们的业务植入这个生态中。这其中的关键是这些基础设施的 开放性与可靠性。过去，人工智能科技是如此的昂贵以至于它们都不值得被使用。今天，人工智能与机器学习触手可得，并且它们 还可以根据个人需求来使用。它们构成了新的商业模型的基础。即使用户不是机器学习专家，它们也可以轻易地将这些模块植入它们的 服务中。这尤其给那些创新型的中小企业带来帮助。它们不必学习复杂的机器学习算法和技术就可以不费大量资金开展试验。

Artificial intelligence helps to satisfy the customer

One of the most advanced areas of application is e-commerce. AI-supported pre-selection mechanisms help companies to free their customers' decision making from complexity. The ultimate goal is customer satisfaction. If there are only three types of toothpaste, the customer can easily pick one and feel good about it. When more than 50 kinds are on offer, the choice becomes complicated. You have to decide, but you're not sure if the decision is the right one. The more possibilities there are, the more difficult it becomes for the customer. Our best-known algorithms come from this field: filtering product suggestions based on one's purchase history of products with similar attributes, or on the behavior of other customers who were interested in similar things. Of course, consistent quality also contributes to the satisfaction of the customer. Intelligent support makes life easier for the provider and the customer. For Amazon Fresh, for example, we have developed algorithms that learn how fresh groceries have to look, how long this state lasts, and when food should no longer be sold. Airlines or rail transport companies could also use this for their quality control by running an algorithm based on the image data of the freight; the algorithm would recognize damaged goods and automatically sort them out. If you can predict demand, you can plan more efficiently In B2B and B2C businesses, it is critical that goods are available quickly. It is for this reason that we at Amazon have developed algorithms that can predict the daily demand of goods. This is particularly complex for fashion goods, which are always available in many different sizes and variations and for which reorder possibilities are very limited. Information about past demand, among others, is fed into our system, as well as fluctuations that can occur with seasonal goods, the effect of special offers, and the sensitivity of customers to price shifts. Today we can predict precisely how many shirts in a certain size and color will be sold on a defined day. We have tackled this issue and made the technology available to other companies as a web service. MyTaxi, for example, benefits from our ML-based service to plan at what time and at which place the customer will need the vehicle.

人工智能帮助服务于客户

人工智能其中一个最有优势的领域就是在电子商务。人工智能所支持的预选机制帮助了企业解决顾客从繁杂的商品里挑选的难题。 其最终的目标就是让顾客满意。假如只有三种牙膏，顾客就可以很轻松的选择一个。相反，如果有超过５０种，那选择就复杂多了。 你不得不做一个决定，但是你又不确定这个决定是否正确。越多的可能性对于顾客俩来说就越困难。来自这个领域一个著名的算法就是： 根据顾客的历史购物记录或者根据喜欢相似商品的顾客行为来过滤出推荐的商品。
当然，保持较高的服务水准也会让客户满意。智能售后同时使得供应商和顾客的生活更加容易。比如亚马逊生鲜，我们已经构建出 一套生鲜食品怎样展示、保险多久以及生么时候不能再卖的算法。航空公司或铁路运输公司同样可以使用寄送过程中的图像数据来用 算法进行质量控制。这些算法可以自动识别损坏的货物，然后自动分拣出它们。
如果你可以预测需求，你也可以更加有效的计划供给。
在B2B和B2C领域，货物的快速供给非常重要。就是因为这个原因，我们在亚马逊开发出可以预测货物需求的算法。这个算法用在 流行时尚物品上时非常复杂。因为这些商品的尺码和款式多种多样，而且重复订购会非常有限。有关过去需求以及其他货物的商品 信息，跟季节性的浮动、特殊折扣的有效性和顾客对于价格浮动敏感性等信息会一同汇入我们的系统。今天，我们可以准确预测出 在某个日期多少尺码和颜色的衬衫会卖出去。我们已经解决了这个问题，并且我们还把它作为网站服务提供给个其他客户。 比如，MyTaxi利用我们提供的基于机器学习的服务可以预测出在某个时刻某个地点顾客会需要叫车。

New division of labor

But AI is much more than just forecasting. In the field of fulfillment, which is relevant for numerous industry sectors, we are thinking of ideas of how AI can contribute the most to taking another step away from a Tayloristic work pattern. Applied in robots, AI can free people from routine activities that are physically difficult and often stressful. Machines are very good at, and sometimes even outperform, tasks that are complicated for a human to do, such as finding the optimal route in a warehouse for a certain number of orders and transporting heavy goods to the point where it is sent to the customer. For supposedly easy tasks, by contrast, the robot is overwhelmed; an example is recognizing a box that has landed on the wrong shelf. So how to bring together the best of both players? By letting intelligent robots learn from humans how to identify the right goods, take on various orders and navigate their way autonomously through the warehouse on the most efficient route. This is how we take away the most tedious part of the task and shift resources to more interaction with the customer. Our client SCDM uses the core idea of freeing up resources for "human" strengths, but in a completely different context. SCDM is a service provider that supports banks and insurance companies with digitization. Using AI, SCDM enables its customers to classify documents that are of very different formats (PDF, Excel or photos), for example a report about the performance of an investment product that contains hundreds of pages. By scanning hundreds of thousands of documents simultaneously, SCDM's algorithm recognizes which document is relevant for a specific request, finds out where relevant data for a specific type of preparation is located, and then extracts the data from the document. As a result, there is less bias and fewer errors in the number crunching, and more time for human interaction with important stakeholders like investors, analysts and other customers. Machine learning in education, medicine and development aid In addition to their potential for things like efficiency and productivity, ML and AI can also be used in education. Duolingo, which offers free language course apps, uses text-to-language algorithms to assess and correct learners' pronunciation. In medicine, AI supports doctors in analyzing X-Ray CTs or MRT images. The World Bank also uses AI in order to implement infrastructure programs, development aid and other measures in a more targeted manner in the future.

工作的新领域

但是人工智能不仅仅是预测这么简单。在跟很多工业领域都相关的包装运送领域，我们正在尝试如何让算法在流水线工作模式中帮助我们。 如果利用机器人，人工智能就可以将人类从那些体力繁重而且紧张的重复中解放出来。机器非常擅长，有时候甚至超越人类于 那些看似复杂的任务，比如为某个订单在仓储库中找出最优的路线，然后将沉重的货物送到将要送给客户的运送点。对于某些 简单的任务，机器人反而大材小用。一个例子就是识别出送到错误架子上的包裹。那么，到底要怎么让两者协同工作呢？ 让智能机器人向人类学习识别正确的货物，执行不同的命令并且自动在仓储库中导航。这就是我们如何释放大多数繁琐的任务来让资源与客户互动的。
我们的客户SCDM就使用这个方法来在一个想回复杂的环境中解放人力。SCDM是一个帮助银行和保险公司数字化的服务提供商。 通过使用人工智能，SCDM使得客户可以将各类格式的文档分类(PDF, Excel或图片），比如有着上百页的投资银行绩效的报告。 通过同时扫描上百页的文档，SCDM的算发会根据要求识别出文档，找到相关数据的某个种类的文档位置，亦或是从文档中提取 数据。这样，这个过程中有更少的偏差和认为疏漏，这也使得人们可以有更多时间与像投资人、分析师或其他客户沟通。
在教育、医疗与应急中的机器学习
除了像提高效率有效性这些潜力，机器学习和人工智能也可以运用在教学当中。Duolingo提供了免费的语言学习软甲。它们 就使用了文本语言转换算法来评估和改正学习者的发音。在医疗中，人工智能帮助医生分析X光片和核磁共振个影像。世界银行 还利用人工智能来实施基础施舍建设与发展援助以及其他未来的方案中。

More room for optimism

Despite all these developments, many people from academia, business and government have a critical view of ML and AI. There have been warnings that a new super-intelligence is jeopardizing our civilization – and these warnings have been effective in attracting publicity. However, neither hysteria nor euphoria should be allowed to get the upper hand in the public debate. What we need instead is a pragmatic-optimistic view of the emerging possibilities. AI enables us to get rid of tasks in our work which damage our health or where machines are better than we are. Not with the goal of making ourselves redundant. Rather, in order to gain more personal and economic freedom – for interpersonal relationships, for our creativity and for everything that we humans can do better than machines. That is what we should strive for. If we don't, we will ultimately forego the economic and societal opportunities that we could have grasped.

乐观者的机遇

尽管有这些发展，很多来自学术、商业与政府机构的人们仍持着怀疑的态度。有些警告说超人工智能正在威胁我们的文明。 这些警告十足的吸引眼球。
尽管这样，过度反应都不应在公众讨论中占据上风。相反，我们要从积极的实践角度看待这些可能性。人工智能帮助我们摆脱了 那些可能伤害我们健康的任务，或者那些比我们做的更好的机器。这绝不是让我们自己变得多余。这更是帮助我们获得个人与经济上 的自由－为了人与人的联系，为了创作，为了一切那些我们可以做的比机器更好的东西。这才是我们所应该追求的。如果我们 不这么做，我绝对会错过那些我们本该抓住的经济与社会上的机遇。
